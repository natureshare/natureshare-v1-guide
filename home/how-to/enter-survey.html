Surveys

How do you enter a Survey into NatureShare?
It could be a list of 20-100 plants seen on one day while walking around a reserve, a list of plants in flower seen on one day on your block of land, or a list of birds (or butterflies) observed while on a formal bird-spotting walk?

It's simple. It is just like any normal Observation but without a photo. You can add as many species to a single Observation as you like.
  • Step 1 - click on 'Add a new observation' in the Observation Section.
  • Step 2 - keep clicking on 'Add a species' until all species names are uploaded to the Observation.
  • Step 3 - click on 'Assign a tag' and enter 'survey', leaving the other box as 'Overall'
    (if it was a survey of plants in flower you should click on 'Assign a tag' a second time and enter 'flower', leaving the other box as 'Overall'.
  • Step 4 - enter a central 'Location' for the survey using the Google-map interface.
As an example, if you or your group does a survey of plants like this survey of a bushfire area you would list dozens of species in the one Observation and assign the Tag 'survey' to all species (ie, the Tag would be 'survey' and you would choose "Overall" in the other box - NatureShare then knows to apply the 'survey' Tag to all species listed in the observation).

As a more complicated example, if you or your group does a survey of plants in flower like this survey at Conglomerate Gully Reserve you again list all the species in the one Observation and assign the Tag 'survey' to all species (ie, the Tag would be 'survey' and you would choose "Overall" in the other box - NatureShare then knows to apply this Tag to all species listed in this observation). This time you would also Tag all species with 'flower' (ie, the Tag would be 'flower' and you would choose "Overall" in the other box - NatureShare then knows to apply the 'flower' Tag to all species listed in the observation).
