What's New

Most recent items listed first:
  • "Fungi spp." added - The species name "Fungi spp." has been added so at least now you can upload fungi observation and name them as Fungi spp. (common name "Fungi"). This allows users to at least re-find those observations (via the search system; NB. they are more easily searchable if they are added to collections). Currently there is no full list of Fungi species names available in Victoria (but we are working on it). June 30 2011.
  • 178 Victorian Spider Species names added - 178 Victorian insect species names have been added, provided to NatureShare by the ABRS team in Canberra. This includes all the species in the ABRS database that are specifically tagged as being recorded in 'Victoria'. There are numerous species not included in this list so if you come across one, contact us. 12 May 2011.
  • 6900 Victorian Insect Species names added - A further 6900 Victorian insect species names have been added, provided to NatureShare after a great deal of work on our behalf by the ABRS team in Canberra. This includes all the species in the ABRS database that are specifically tagged as being recorded in 'Victoria'. There are numerous species not included in this list so if you come across one, contact us. See also the next item. 12 May 2011.
  • Insect provisional names added to NatureShare - If you don't know what insect species you've seen but you know what general category of insects it is in, there are some 'provisional names' that have been added to NatureShare to help you. For example, you can simply enter the common name you are familiar with, such as 'beetle' (Coleoptera spp.), 'weevil', 'praying mantis', 'katydid', 'mole cricket', 'dragonfly', 'butterfly', etc and chances are it is in NatureShare. Just about every generic name is in NatureShare. If you ever find out the species name or hone in on a more specific name (eg. which family it is in), that can changed later. This is also a stop-gap until more comprehensive species lists become available. Click on this link to see all the generic names for insects. The names in this list are largely provided in the excellent and widely available book: Zborowski, P. and Storey, R. (2010) A Field Guide to Insects in Australia. Hew Holland. ... with additional material accessed via the ANIC CSIRO website. Apr 1 2011.
  • Victorian Grasshopper Species added to NatureShare - as far as we know, at the time of publishing, this is the most comprehensive checklist available for grasshoppers in Victoria (contact us if you know differently) - see the grasshopper category. 30 Mar 2011.
  • 'Provisional-names' Category added - what are provisional names? Examples include Geranium spp., Convolvulus erubescens spp. agg., Acacia acinacea s.l. and Goodia lotifolia (which has two subspecies - the name 'Goodia lotifolia' is included as an option for those people who don't know which subspecies they see). Basically, provisional names are names in NatureShare that are there to help users enter a name even if they don't know precisely what species or subspecies or variety it is. For example, if you know your observation is a grasshopper but don't the species you can enter 'Caelifera spp.' (or indeed just 'grasshopper', which is the generic common name for Caelifera spp.). This category is useful, for example, when compiling a collection, you can search for names in the collection that are provisional names; ie. those that require a bit more work to identify precisely which species or subspecies it is. See the 'provisional-name' category. Soon we are hoping to add 'similar species' for all relevant provisional-names so the user can see which species or subspecies, etc, relate to each provisional names (this process is underway now). 30 Mar 2011.
  • 'Lily' category added - all 'lilies' are no longer in a nice easy Liliaceae family so in a way to search for lilies within a collection wasn't easy, until now. We have now added a 'Lily' category so you can now search for lilies using the 'category' part of the advanced search. 30 Mar 2011.
  • Important addition to help section ...
    "I know the plant's name but the name doesn't come up in NatureShare" - the most likely reason for this is the plant name has changed. To find out the new name you can either:
    • try entering the common name in the 'Quick Search' system (or do an advanced search for the common name if more than one plant has the same common name).
    • have a look for the name you know in the Victorian Plant Census - the old census is on-line and the next one to come out should be available (very cheaply) via the Royal Botanic Gardens website. The census will tell you, for example:
      • Bracteantha bracteata = Xerochrysum bracteatum
      • Brachyscome cardiocarpa = Allittia cardiocarpa
      • Calectasia cyanea = Calectasia intermedia
      • etc
  • 'Castlemaine Plant Database' - added with 52 Collections including plant lists for 52 reserves/areas in the Castlemaine district (from Trentham to Mandurang) - amounting to over 10,000 species records. This all thanks to the amazing work of Ern Perkins and the Castlemaine Field Nats, and a considerable amount of work on the NatureShare side (many thanks to Helen Snee in particular). Mar 2-10 2011.
  • 'What's New' and 'Coming Soon' - pages added to this 'Help & Documentation' wiki/site - see menu at the top of the page. 5 Mar 2011.
  • Important notice added to help site "Important: The species names for all of the plants, mammals, birds, frogs, snakes & lizards, butterflies and dragonflies known to occur in Victoria are in the NatureShare database. Some moth names are also in the database. Observations for all the species in these groups are now possible in NatureShare. Other species groups will come on-line in the near future - keep an eye on 'What's New'!" - 4 Mar 2011.
