Butterflies

There are about 140 butterfly species recorded in Victoria. For most local area collections there are likely to be less than 40 species so setting up local collections is the best way to work out what is in that area (eg. Butterflies of Riddells Creek). For further help, for any local or Victoria-wide search, the colour(s) of the wing will usually be enough to narrow the search to a small number of species. The main searchable attributes for butterflies that have currently being entered are:
  • wing colour; eg. 'wing: blue', 'wing: yellow', etc.
Unlike moths, both sides of the wing is sometimes required for identification. Wing colour is generally enough to find your butterfly if a local 'collection' is available. If the wing is multi-coloured, then it is often best to split this information into separate attributes; eg. if a wing is red, black and white, rather than one attribute, "wing: red-black-white", searching often works better if three attributes are added - ie. "wing: red", "wing: black" and "wing: white". Similarly, if the colour of the wing can vary or can be interpreted in different ways (eg. yellow-orange, cream-white, cream-yellow, grey-black) then it is best to split this into separate attributes (eg. rather than "wing: yellow-orange", enter two attributes separately as "wing: orange" and "wing: yellow").

Seeing the butterfly pictured right in Riddells Creek, an advanced search might include:
'wing: orange AND wing: black AND wing: white AND collection: Butterflies of Riddells Creek' or
'wing: brown AND wing: white' for the same collection.
Using wing colours alone is an efficient way to work out any species you see in Victoria. Once you get to know butterfly species, you will learn that limiting searches to 'skipper and dart' butterflies or excluding skipper and dart butterflies makes a significant difference to a search (family: hesperiidae - which is a classification - accounts for about 30% of Victoria's butterfly species). For example, excluding skippers and darts from the above search gives better results:
'wing: brown AND wing: white AND exclude family: hespiriidae'

HOW YOU CAN HELP: If you find a butterfly species without these basic attributes entered in NatureShare, please enter them (especially when setting up a new butterfly 'collection' or when a new butterfly is added to a collection because without these attributes the collection cannot be searched properly). Attributes presented here are not meant to be exhaustive - if you want to enter other attributes, NatureShare allows this - anything is possible.

Other attributes that have been used with butterflies include:
  • Food-plant; eg. "food-plant: acacia-melanoxylon" - but only if this is specifically observed in Victoria (ie. not just read from a book)
  • Caterpillar; eg. "caterpillar: attendant-ants", "caterpillar: hairy" - see more caterpillar attributes.

Butterflies not yet recorded in Victoria
The following butterflies are likely to occur in Victoria but they haven't been recorded yet. If you see, photograph or collect these butterflies in Victoria please contact the NatureShare team.
  • Herimosa albovenata albovenata (White-veined Sand-skipper, White-veined Skipper - with prominent white veins). It is most likely to be seen in East Gippsland (records near Bombala, NSW) or less likely in the Mallee region (records near Tintinarra, SA). The food plant in Victoria is likely to be Austrostipa scabra, A. semibarbata and A. eremophila (all are Spear Grass species).
  • Ocybadistes walkeri hypochlora (Southern Dart, Green Grass-dart, Yellow-banded Dart). This butterfly has been recorded near Naracoorte in SA. Native food plant in Victoria is likely to be Imperata cylindrica (Blady Grass) and possibly Cynodon dactylon var. pulchellus (Native Couch) but it has also recorded feeding on many non-native (weed) grasses.
