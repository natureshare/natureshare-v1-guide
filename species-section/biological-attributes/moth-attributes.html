The Species Section > Biological Attributes >

Moths

There are over 2000 moth species in Victoria. For any local search, the colour(s) of the wing will often be enough to narrow the search to a feasible number of species. The main searchable attributes for moths that are currently being entered are:
  • wing colour; eg. 'wing: green', 'wing: yellow', 'wing: white', etc.
    If the wing is multi-coloured, then it is often best to split this colour information into separate attributes; eg. if a wing is red, black and white, rather than one attribute, "wing: red-black-white", searching often works better if three attributes are added - ie. "wing: red", "wing: black" and "wing: white". Similarly, if the colour of the wing can vary or can be interpreted in different ways (eg. yellow-orange, cream-white, cream-yellow, grey-black) then it is best to split this into separate attributes (eg. rather than "wing: grey-black", enter two attributes separately as "wing: grey" and "wing: black").
    ("wing" in this sense means the wing-upperside which is usually distinctive and is most often seen - see right - the wing underside is usually not observed, unless viewing on windows inside a house, and usually indistinct, unlike in butterflies)
  • number of colours (including shades) on wing; 3 options are:
    • 'wing: one-coloured',
    • 'wing: two-coloured' or
    • 'wing: multi-coloured'.
The best search is done by listing the colours you see on the wing; eg.
  • a) wing: white AND wing: one-coloured (example right is Trichiocercus sparshalli - Sparshalli Moth);
  • b) wing: white AND wing: brown AND wing: two-coloured).

HOW YOU CAN HELP: If you find a moth species without these basic attributes entered in NatureShare, please enter them (especially when setting up a new moth 'collection' or when a new moth is added to a collection because without these attributes the collection cannot be searched). Attributes presented here are not meant to be exhaustive - if you want to enter other attributes that you know will help, NatureShare allows this - anything is possible.

Examples of other search items, when distinctive, include:
  • colour of spots; eg. 'spots: red'.
  • colour of lines; eg. 'line: brown'.
  • wing fringe; eg. 'wing-fringe: chequered', 'wing-fringe: pink'.
  • wing underside colour; eg. 'wing-underside: red'.
  • palp features; eg. 'palp: hairy', 'palp: upturned'.
  • body features; eg. 'body: upturned'.
  • head features; eg. 'head-hair: black', 'blad-spot: red' - see picture top right.
HOW YOU CAN HELP: If you find a moth species that has a distinctive feature/attribute(s), please enter them.
