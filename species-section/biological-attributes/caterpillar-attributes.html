Caterpillars

This section applies to caterpillars of butterflies and moths (order: lepidoptera). In releative terms, there is very little information available to help identify caterpillars so many observations may not have species attached to them.

For any local search, the main/stand-out colour of the body and whether the caterpillar is hairy may be enough to narrow the search down to a feasible number of species. If the food plant is known this could also be used in the search. Because caterpillars and their adults have the same species name, it is easier to begin all caterpillar physical attributes with "caterpillar: ...". So, the main attributes are:
  • body colour; eg. "caterpillar: red", "caterpillar: black", etc. If the body is multi-coloured, then it is often best to split this information into separate attributes; eg. if a body is yellow and black, rather than one attribute, "caterpillar: yellow-black", searching often works better if two attributes are added - ie. "caterpillar: yellow" and "caterpillar: black". Similarly, if the colour of the caterpillar can vary or can be interpreted in different ways (eg. yellow-orange, cream-white, cream-yellow, purple-mauve) then it is best to split this into separate attributes (eg. rather than "caterpillar: cream-yellow", enter two attributes separately as "caterpillar: cream" and "caterpillar: yellow").
  • body hair; eg. "caterpillar: hairy" (NB. caterpillars that are not hairy don't necessarily need anything listing because the search can 'exclude' "caterpillar: hairy").
  • attendant ants; eg. "caterpillar: attendant-ants" - see picture right of Jalmenus icilius, Amethyst Hairstreak butterfly (NB. caterpillars that have no attendant ants don't necessarily need anything listing because the search can 'exclude' "caterpillar: attendant-ants").
  • food plant; eg. "food-plant: Acacia-melanoxylon", "food-plant: Lomandra-filiformis-coriacea" - only if this is specifically observed in Victoria (ie. not just read from a book)
HOW YOU CAN HELP: If you find a caterpillar without these basic attributes entered in NatureShare, please enter them.

The attributes presented here are not the only ones that can help with caterpillar identification - if you want to enter other attributes, NatureShare allows this - anything is possible.
